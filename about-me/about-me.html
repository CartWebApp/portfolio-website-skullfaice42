About Me

I have consistently demonstrated academic excellence throughout my high school career. During both my junior year (2023–2024) and senior year, I was honored with the Principal’s Honor Roll. In my freshman and sophomore years, I maintained a GPA above 3.0, reflecting a strong and steady academic performance. Currently, I hold the distinction of being the valedictorian at Fresno High School.
Outside of academics, I have a wide range of interests that reflect both creativity and problem solving. I enjoy applying mathematical concepts—particularly formulas related to construction and engineering—to practical tasks around the house, such as repairing fences or troubleshooting broken machines using power tools. In my free time, I find origami to be a calming and meditative activity that also sharpens my focus and precision. I'm also passionate about 3D modeling and printing, which allows me to bring digital designs into the physical world. Additionally, I enjoy learning new skills and often use Python programming to support and enhance my projects.
After graduating from high school, I plan to pursue a Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering. My goal is to deepen my understanding of how systems work and to gain the skills necessary to design and build technologies that simplify everyday life. I am driven by a desire to create solutions that automate repetitive or tedious tasks, allowing people to focus more on the meaningful and enjoyable aspects of life. Through continuous learning and innovation, I hope to contribute to a future where technology enhances both productivity and personal well being.
